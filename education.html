{ My Education }

Education is the building blocks of my journey, providing me with the knowledge and skills that serve as the foundation for my pursuits. Fueled by passion for continuous learning and intellectual growth, I have dedicated myself in acquiring competencies that has equipped me to navigate this present dynamic landscape. Here is a timeline into my educational background, where each milestone has been a stepping stone towards realizing my potential and contributing meaningfully to the world around me.




  • July 2023 to December 2023
    Introduction to Computer Science
    HarvardX
    Harvard's CS50, formally known as "Introduction to Computer Science," is a challenging computer science course offered online for free by Harvard University. This course helped me understand basics of computer science and programming.

    CS50 taught me a wide array of topics, including algorithms, software development, web development, databases, and computer systems. The course emphasized on my problem-solving, algorithmic thinking, and the development of computational skills using languages like C, Python, and SQL. CS50 is well-regarded for its engaging lectures and hands-on projects.
  • 2016-2019
    Masters in Manufacturing Engineering and Management
    Completed 33 Units
    Saint Louis University
    This is a postgraduate program that focuses on advanced concepts and practices in the field of manufacturing and a blend of business principles to prepare professionals for leadership roles in technical organizations. This program is a tie-up course tailored by Saint Lous University and Moog Controls Corporation.

    It covers a broad range of topics including accounting, organizational development, lean management, production planning, machining tools and processes, geometrical dimensioning and tolerance, engineering measurements, organizational behavior, financial management, and engineering materials testing.
  • 2009-2014
    Bachelor of Science in Electronics Engineering
    Instrumentations Track
    Saint Louis University
    My bachelor’s degree, helped me gain experiences, honed skills, and acquired comprehensive knowledge, and practical applications within electronics engineering. The course equipped me with foundations in circuit design, signal processing, programming, and control systems. Also, projects and laboratory work allowed me to apply theoretical knowledge to real-world scenarios, enhancing my problem-solving abilities.

    Projects during my undergrad are: line following robot, Tic-Tac-Toe Game using logic gate IC’s, three axes pick and place robot, and a project study entitled: “Determination of Blood Alcohol Concentration and Alcohol Monitoring using an Integrated Alcohol Detection System” which was showcased at the 7th Northern Luzon Research Congress on Computing and Information Sciences, held March 10-12, 2014.
  • 2005-2009
    Special Science Class
    Pines City National High School
    I successfully completed the Special Science Program (SSP), where my passion for science and mathematics was nurtured through a specialized curriculum of the Department of Education. The selective admission process, entrance exams, and academic challenges prepared me for higher education in STEM fields, while engaging extracurricular activities like science fairs and math competitions enriched my overall learning experience.
